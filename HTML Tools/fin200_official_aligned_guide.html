Chapter 2

Theme: Introduction to valuation and the time value of money.

Pages 1-13

Part 2.1: Determining the Future Value of an Investment

  • Learning objective: determine the future value of an investment made today.
  • Future value depends on three core inputs: present value, interest rate, and time.
  • The chapter clearly distinguishes between simple interest and compounded interest.
  • The course emphasis is on compounded interest.
  • The slides include both single-period investment and multi-period investment examples.
  • Excel method shown in the chapter: =FV(rate, nper, 0, -pv).
  • The chapter also includes a financial calculator setup for FV.
Exam-aligned reminder: Chapter 2 is not only about the idea of future value. It explicitly includes simple vs. compounded interest, FV in Excel, and FV using a financial calculator.
Pages 14-23

Part 2.2: Determining the Present Value of Future Cash Flows

  • Learning objective: determine the present value of cash to be received at a future date.
  • Topics covered: present value concept, single-period PV, multi-period PV.
  • Excel method shown in the chapter: =PV(rate, nper, 0, fv).
  • The chapter also introduces the idea of a perpetuity.
  • Importance of present value is tied to evaluating investments and comparing cash flows.
Pages 24-32

Part 2.3: Finding the Return on an Investment

  • Learning objective: find the return on an investment.
  • The chapter presents return on investment (ROI) as a percentage measure of gain or loss relative to the amount invested.
  • Excel method shown in the chapter: =RATE(nper, 0, -pv, fv).
  • This section is directly relevant for annualized return questions.
Pages 33-40

Part 2.4: Calculating Investment Duration

  • Learning objective: calculate how long it takes an investment to reach a desired value.
  • The chapter frames this as a time-to-target problem.
  • Excel method shown in the chapter: =NPER(rate, 0, -pv, fv).
  • This section supports planning and timeline questions.

Chapter 3

Theme: Multiple cash flows, annuities, loan calculations, amortization, and quoted rates.

Pages 1-12

Part 3.1: Future and Present Value of Multiple Cash Flows

  • The chapter begins with FV of multiple cash flows, then PV of multiple cash flows.
  • An example using Al-Jazira Capital appears for both FV and PV.
  • Excel guidance for FV: calculate the future value of each cash flow and sum them.
  • Excel guidance for PV: use =NPV(rate, cashflow_range) for the total present value.
  • The chapter also includes financial calculator workflows.
Pages 13-18

Part 3.2: Present and Future Value of Annuities

  • The chapter defines an annuity as equal payments over a specific period.
  • It explicitly distinguishes between ordinary annuity and annuity due.
  • It notes that in Excel, annuity due uses type=1 and ordinary annuity uses type=0.
  • The chapter includes real-world examples such as rent and car payments.
Pages 19-32

Part 3.3: Calculating Loan Payments and Finding Interest Rates

  • The chapter covers loan payment calculation using PMT.
  • Example: Dar Al-Arkan real estate development company.
  • Excel PMT example: =PMT(rate, nper, -pv).
  • The chapter then shows how to find an unknown interest rate using RATE.
  • Excel RATE example: =RATE(nper, pmt, pv, 0, 0).
Pages 33-46

Part 3.4: Loan Amortization

  • The chapter explains how loans are amortized or paid off.
  • It uses PMT again, then builds an amortization schedule.
  • The schedule breaks each payment into interest, principal, and remaining balance.
  • The chapter includes spreadsheet creation steps for an amortization schedule.
Pages 47-55

Part 3.5: Quoting and Misquoting Interest Rates

  • This section explains APR and EAR.
  • APR does not account for compounding within the year.
  • EAR does account for compounding and is more accurate for comparison.
  • Excel function shown in the chapter: =EFFECT(apr, periods_per_year).

Chapter 4

Theme: Bond features, pricing, yields, ratings, inflation effects, and the term structure of interest rates.

Pages 1-8

Part 4.1: Bond Features and Types

  • The chapter defines what a bond is.
  • Basic bond features include face value, coupon payment, coupon rate, maturity date, and issuer.
  • Types of bonds shown: treasury bonds, municipal bonds, corporate bonds, and zero-coupon bonds.
  • The chapter also introduces bonds with options, especially the call provision.
Pages 9-20

Part 4.2: Bond Values and Yields and Why They Fluctuate

  • Bond price is defined as the present value of future cash flows.
  • Excel bond price example shown: =PV(required_return, years, coupon_payment, face_value, 0).
  • The chapter then explains bond yield and shows how to calculate it with RATE.
  • It clearly distinguishes yield from coupon rate.
  • It covers premium bonds, discount bonds, and bonds at par.
  • It explains how bond prices move toward par as maturity approaches.
  • It covers market interest rates, inflation expectations, and other factors affecting bond prices and yields.
Pages 21-30

Part 4.3: Bond Ratings and What They Mean

  • The chapter includes rating agencies: Moody's, S&P, and Fitch.
  • It presents rating scales and the distinction between investment grade and non-investment grade (junk bonds).
  • It explains the meaning of ratings and factors considered by rating agencies.
Pages 31-42

Part 4.4: The Impact of Inflation on Interest Rates

  • The chapter explains inflation, central bank response, and bond value effects.
  • It introduces the Fisher Effect.
  • It distinguishes between nominal interest rate, real interest rate, and inflation rate.
Pages 43-53

Part 4.5: Term Structure of Interest Rates and Determinants of Bond Yields

  • The chapter explains the term structure through the yield curve.
  • It gives an upward-sloping yield curve example.
  • It covers determinants of bond yields, including interest rate risk, inflation expectations, credit risk, liquidity risk, and economic conditions.

Chapter 5

Theme: Stock valuation, stock valuation multiples, corporate voting systems, and stock market operation.

Pages 1-10

Part 5.1: Stock Valuation from Dividends and Dividend Growth

  • The chapter begins with common stock valuation and explains why stock valuation is more complex than bond valuation.

  • It also includes the Sustainable Growth Rate (SGR) and its key components such as retention ratio and ROE.

Part 5.3: Corporate Directors and Voting Systems

Part 5.4: How Stock Markets Work

  • The chapter introduces the Tadawul and market participants.
